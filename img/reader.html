I A Sample Chapter

Folio I · i

The morning came in pale and uncommitted, the way mornings do when no one is watching them arrive. Below the window the street went about its small business — a cart, a closed door, a lamp left burning past its use.

She had not meant to stop here. People rarely do. But the hour was thin and the road was long, and somewhere a bell was counting something out.

A figure waited at the corner, patient as weather.

Folio I · ii

She did not know why she had stopped. People did not stop on this street; stopping was how a place learned your face.

I should keep walking. I don't know this place. So why does the corner feel like a question left open?

Beneath the thought, quieter, came a second feeling she could not name — not fear exactly, but its near relation, the one that arrives early and stays too long.

The figure did not move. The lamp did not gutter. Somewhere, patiently, the bell kept its count.

A note for the margins: none of this is the real story. The names are borrowed, the scene invented — a stand-in, so the shape of the thing can be shown without giving the thing away.

She stayed, because staying was easier than the alternative, and watched a stranger she did not know begin to say something she did not yet understand.

Above the street, the morning went on arriving — pale, patient, in no particular hurry.
